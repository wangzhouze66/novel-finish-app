# Dragon Raja / Long Zu Hybrid Route v1

This document creates the first mature hybrid ending route for the *Dragon Raja / Long Zu* ending experiment. It is an outline-level route only. It is not a final chapter, not final prose, and not a substitute for later final-volume, chapter, and scene outlining.

The route uses the evaluation result that **Hybrid A+C with B/D tonal safeguards** is the strongest immediate expansion direction: A supplies closure and accountability, C supplies visible epic climax and ensemble scale, B supplies cost pressure, D supplies open resonance, and E supplies restrained emotional compensation where it strengthens character closure without reversing loss.

---

## 1. Hybrid route identity

### Route name

**`Hybrid Route v1 — 雨后王座无人 / The Empty Throne After Rain`**

### Inherited base routes

- **Primary structure:** A. Complete closure ending + C. Epic battle ending.
- **Cost discipline:** B. Tragic fate ending.
- **Final atmosphere:** D. Open resonance ending.
- **Selective emotional repair:** E. Emotional compensation ending.

### Route type

**Decisive closure / epic final-war hybrid with tragic cost, controlled mystery, and bounded emotional compensation.**

### Central thesis

The world can be saved only when Lu Mingfei refuses every system that tries to turn lonely children into payment: the dragon throne, the bargain, Odin's memory machine, the Secret Party's secrecy, and the old fantasy that power can return everything that was lost.

### Emotional thesis

The ending should feel like a graduation after an apocalypse: the rain stops, the campus lights remain on, some names are finally spoken aloud, and the person who most wanted to be ordinary can no longer return to being the boy he was.

### Why this hybrid is stronger than a single-route ending

- It keeps **A's closure strength** without letting the ending become an administrative explanation of dragon governance.
- It keeps **C's epic scale** without turning the climax into pure power fantasy.
- It imports **B's tragic pressure** so victory has a visible bill: memory, future, innocence, institutional comfort, and the possibility of simple return.
- It imports **D's resonance** so the highest myths are not over-explained after their operational rules are settled.
- It imports **E's compensation** only as recognition, farewell, chosen memory, and small human repair, never as convenient resurrection or romantic reward.

## 2. Core ending philosophy

### What must be closed

- **Lu Mingfei's final self-definition:** he must make an active final choice and define himself by agency rather than by being chosen, pitied, purchased, or crowned.
- **Lu Mingze's bond and bargain cost:** the route must name what the bargain demands and what Lu Mingze has been protecting, manipulating, and fearing.
- **Odin's rules and Chu Zihang's erased-history wound:** Odin must be defeated as a fate/memory mechanism, and Chu must recover enough truth to become witness rather than victim.
- **Nono's agency:** she must not become a prize, symbolic reward, or emotional medicine for Lu Mingfei.
- **Dragon-world operating order:** the final state of bloodline, succession, secrecy, and human-dragon coexistence must be legible.
- **No-resurrection ethics:** death and erasure may leave echoes, records, or farewells, but no route mechanism should cheaply restore the dead.

### What can remain resonant or mysterious

- The exact metaphysical origin of the Black King can remain mythic after the succession rules are clarified.
- Lu Mingze's ultimate ontology can retain a final shimmer: independent being, fate-shadow, split inheritance, or impossible younger brother, as long as his motive and cost are emotionally clear.
- Odin's earliest origin can remain partially masked once his function, motive, and defeat condition are fair.
- The distant future of all dragon bloodline anomalies can remain open if the immediate order is stabilized and ethically constrained.
- The final image may allow uncertainty about whether memory, fate, or love leaves a harmless echo after the war.

### What emotional debt must be paid

- Lu Mingfei must be recognized without being rewarded by possession, resurrection, or effortless happiness.
- Lu Mingze must be mourned as more than a trickster device.
- Chu Zihang must recover public dignity for erased lives, not only personal revenge.
- Erii's absence must be honored as a boundary: loving the dead means refusing to counterfeit them.
- Caesar and Nono must receive adult choices that prove they are not trapped inside earlier romantic or family roles.
- Kassel must acknowledge that its jokes, uniforms, missions, and heroism were built beside real exploitation of children.

### What kind of cost the ending requires

The cost should be **specific, distributed, and irreversible**:

- Lu Mingfei refuses the throne but loses the possibility of returning to his old anonymous life.
- Lu Mingze is released from the bargain structure but cannot remain beside Lu Mingfei in his old form.
- Chu regains truth but must live as one of its carriers.
- Caesar breaks inherited power but inherits public responsibility.
- Nono keeps freedom but must choose what kind of care can survive without possession.
- Kassel survives, but not as an innocent academy.
- Dragon bloodline continues, but no longer as an unquestioned aristocracy or secret weapon economy.

### What kind of aftertaste the ending should leave

The aftertaste should be **rain-washed, spacious, and aching rather than triumphant**: a world saved at dawn, a classroom with repaired windows, a roster that includes the erased, a phone that no longer promises miracles, and an empty throne that matters because no one sits on it.

## 3. Main conflict resolution

### Lu Mingfei's final choice

Lu Mingfei enters the final conflict with the legal, mythic, and emotional right to claim the dragon throne or use the bargain to end the war by becoming the new center of the cycle. His final choice is to **refuse both crown and victimhood**:

- He accepts responsibility for ending the immediate apocalypse.
- He refuses to become the Black King's successor, the White King's corrective instrument, or the Secret Party's ultimate weapon.
- He uses the last bargain not to resurrect the lost or enthrone himself, but to sever the succession engine and make the cost visible to everyone who benefited from secrecy.
- He survives physically if the user later chooses the compensation-leaning path, but he does not survive unchanged: his bloodline status, memories, public identity, and ordinary future are altered.

### Lu Mingze's resolution

Lu Mingze is handled as a **bound fate-shadow / impossible brother / contract-keeper** whose emotional motive is clear even if metaphysics retains some haze.

- The route reveals that the bargain was tied to a Black/White succession mechanism that tried to use Lu Mingfei as the next throne vessel.
- Lu Mingze has protected Lu Mingfei partly to preserve the vessel and partly because affection grew inside the contract until it became indistinguishable from betrayal.
- The final price demands Lu Mingfei's name, memory, throne-body, or ordinary future.
- Lu Mingfei changes the bargain's direction: instead of paying himself into the throne, he pays by dismantling the throne's claim over him.
- Lu Mingze is released from being the collector. His ending is disappearance, transformation, or a single bounded echo, not conventional survival.

### Black King / White King / dragon-world order

The Black King and White King function as **mythic origin pressures and inheritance engines**, not just final bosses.

- The ending clarifies the operational rule: the old dragon order survives through recurrence, succession, and bodies forced to carry mythic debt.
- The Black King represents domination, origin, return, and the temptation to settle suffering through absolute power.
- The White King represents rebellion, purification, correction, and the danger that even resistance can become another throne.
- Lu Mingfei's refusal breaks the immediate succession engine and prevents a new absolute sovereign from forming.
- Dragon bloodline remains real, dangerous, and partially mysterious, but it moves into ethical stabilization rather than annihilation or worship.

### Kassel College and Secret Party

Kassel survives as a transformed institution, while the Secret Party loses its unquestioned monopoly.

- Kassel is re-founded as a protective research academy with memorial obligations, student oversight, and limits on using students as expendable weapons.
- The Secret Party is exposed in enough detail to answer its decisive crimes, but not every hidden family history is catalogued.
- Caesar helps break Gattuso-style hereditary immunity from within the elite order.
- Anjou's legacy becomes double-edged: his war mattered, but his methods cannot become the future's moral foundation.

### Human-dragon future order

The final order is **provisional peace with accountable guardianship**.

- Hybrids are not erased or purified into normality.
- Dragon power is regulated as curse, inheritance, and responsibility rather than noble entitlement.
- The public world remains largely protected from immediate panic, but total secrecy ends among those whose lives are governed by the truth.
- The route avoids utopia: some factions resist, some archives remain missing, and future conflicts are possible, but the old cycle is broken for at least one generation.

### Fate, memory, sacrifice, and lost-time motifs

- **Fate:** becomes a mechanism that can be refused only by a personal choice made at real cost.
- **Memory:** becomes resistance; restored names and chosen witnesses matter more than total exposition.
- **Sacrifice:** is morally legible because the route states what is lost, who benefits, and what cannot be restored.
- **Lost time:** is not repaired; the ending honors it through graduation imagery, empty seats, unanswered calls, and older survivors who cannot return to their younger selves.

## 4. Major character final arcs

### Lu Mingfei

- **Final emotional position:** Recognized, lonely, no longer self-erasing; still awkward in small human ways, but unable to pretend he is only the boy who arrived late.
- **Story function in the ending:** Final agent who refuses throne, victimhood, and resurrection temptation.
- **Unresolved arc closure:** Closes MR-01, CA-01, MT-06, ET-05 by making his identity a chosen refusal rather than a revealed destiny alone.
- **Possible cost:** Loss of ordinary anonymity, partial memory scar, altered bloodline status, separation from Lu Mingze, and inability to recover his lost youth.
- **Style note for portrayal:** Keep self-mockery, hesitation, and small absurd observations around him; his courage should feel like a tired boy choosing anyway, not a sudden emperor voice.

### Lu Mingze

- **Final emotional position:** Tender and cruel until the end; relieved from the collector role, but unable to remain as ordinary family.
- **Story function in the ending:** The bargain's face, the emotional contradiction of protection-as-coercion, and the route's deepest sacrifice pressure.
- **Unresolved arc closure:** Closes MR-02, MT-01, MT-02 by explaining motive and price while preserving final ontological mystery.
- **Possible cost:** Disappearance, transformation into harmless memory echo, loss of individuality, or release into a state Lu Mingfei cannot follow.
- **Style note for portrayal:** Use teasing, chesslike cruelty, sudden quiet, and one emotionally naked beat; do not turn him into a simple villain or sentimental child.

### Chu Zihang

- **Final emotional position:** No longer erased, not healed, but anchored by truth and witness duty.
- **Story function in the ending:** Breaks Odin's memory mechanism and proves that remembrance is action.
- **Unresolved arc closure:** Closes MR-03, MR-04, CA-03, WT-03, MT-03, MT-04 through public record and personal confrontation.
- **Possible cost:** He must carry memories others cannot bear; revenge ends, but grief remains practical and daily.
- **Style note for portrayal:** Keep restraint, precision, and action over confession; his emotion should show in what he records, protects, or refuses to forget.

### Caesar

- **Final emotional position:** Proud but less performative; capable of surrendering inherited advantage without surrendering grandeur.
- **Story function in the ending:** Opens the elite/political lock and turns aristocratic spectacle into accountable leadership.
- **Unresolved arc closure:** Closes SR-01, CA-04, part of SR-04 by breaking Gattuso immunity and choosing loyalty over family script.
- **Possible cost:** Family rupture, political exile, loss of status, or becoming responsible for reforms that will not love him back.
- **Style note for portrayal:** Give him stylish decisiveness and dry humor, but let maturity appear through restraint rather than louder declarations.

### Nono

- **Final emotional position:** Free, perceptive, wounded, and unwilling to let anyone turn her care into ownership.
- **Story function in the ending:** Tests whether the ending can grant recognition without making her a romantic prize.
- **Unresolved arc closure:** Closes MR-06, CA-05, ET-02 by giving her an active choice in the postwar order and an emotionally honest but non-possessive relationship with Lu Mingfei.
- **Possible cost:** She must give care without saving Lu Mingfei from all loneliness; she may choose a road that does not satisfy either romantic camp completely.
- **Style note for portrayal:** Use teasing and sudden insight; keep her mobile, sharp, emotionally evasive, and self-owned.

### Erii's emotional legacy

- **Final emotional position:** Absent but protected; her memory becomes a moral boundary and a measure of ordinary beauty.
- **Story function in the ending:** Prevents resurrection convenience and reminds Lu Mingfei what power must not counterfeit.
- **Unresolved arc closure:** Closes SR-03, CA-06, MT-05 by establishing no true reversal and allowing only memory, record, and farewell.
- **Possible cost:** No return, no replacement, no corrected romance; only recognition that her brief ordinary dream mattered.
- **Style note for portrayal:** Use gentle images: sea-red light, a small object, a written name, a protected room, or silence; never use her only as pain fuel.

### Principal Anjou

- **Final emotional position:** Old soldier at the end of his war, elegant, vindicated in part, judged in part.
- **Story function in the ending:** Bridges heroic resistance and institutional guilt.
- **Unresolved arc closure:** Closes SR-02, CA-07 by letting his vengeance reach an endpoint while refusing to canonize all his methods.
- **Possible cost:** Death, retirement, loss of command, or survival into accountability.
- **Style note for portrayal:** Keep polish, irony, historical weight, and blade-like explanation; avoid turning him into a pure mentor or pure culprit.

### Odin

- **Final emotional position:** Defeated as mechanism, still frightening as symbol until the moment memory stops feeding him.
- **Story function in the ending:** Embodies erased history, fatal pursuit, and the enemy that turns personal loss into cosmic inevitability.
- **Unresolved arc closure:** Closes MR-03, CA-08, MT-03 by clarifying function and motive while leaving earliest origin partly masked.
- **Possible cost:** Odin's defeat requires restored witnesses and may expose lies that protected survivors from grief.
- **Style note for portrayal:** Keep him sparse, omen-like, and more presence than personality; do not over-humanize him.

### Black King / White King symbolic presence

- **Final emotional position:** Not emotionally human; present as origin pressure, inheritance temptation, and the shadow of absolute solutions.
- **Story function in the ending:** Gives mythic scale to Lu Mingfei's refusal and the empty-throne image.
- **Unresolved arc closure:** Closes WT-02, SR-06, CA-09 enough to settle succession, while leaving CO-01 partially open.
- **Possible cost:** Breaking the succession engine may destroy ancient knowledge, powers, or forms of dragon beauty along with tyranny.
- **Style note for portrayal:** Use ritual, spare symbolic language and images; avoid boss-mechanic reduction.

### Other key supporting figures

- **Final emotional position:** Survivors, witnesses, reformers, memorial names, and ordinary people who prove the world is larger than the main cast.
- **Story function in the ending:** Protect side-character dignity without creating an exhaustive epilogue roll call.
- **Unresolved arc closure:** Addresses CA-10, CO-04 through selected glimpses, battlefield roles, and memorial texture.
- **Possible cost:** Some futures remain unknown; some beloved minor figures receive image-based rather than plot-heavy closure.
- **Style note for portrayal:** Use concise glimpses: a repaired dorm, a cafeteria joke, a name on a roster, a student guarding an archive, a teacher turning off lights.

## 7. Key emotional scenes

### Scene 1 — Cafeteria joke before the last mobilization

- **Scene purpose:** Preserve youthful academy tone before final-war pressure.
- **Characters involved:** Lu Mingfei, Nono, Chu, Caesar, supporting Kassel students.
- **Emotional subtext:** They perform normality because everyone understands it may be the last ordinary meal.
- **Atmosphere:** Fluorescent lights, rain on windows, cheap food, tactical screens in the background.
- **Symbolic image:** A joke written on a napkin beside a mission briefing.
- **What changes after the scene:** The ensemble leaves school-life safety and enters final-war mode.
- **Risk of mishandling:** Humor could feel flippant unless fear is visible underneath.

### Scene 2 — Lu Mingze names the debt without sounding victorious

- **Scene purpose:** Pay off the bargain's emotional and narrative weight.
- **Characters involved:** Lu Mingfei, Lu Mingze.
- **Emotional subtext:** Lu Mingze wants Lu Mingfei to hate him because hatred would make farewell easier.
- **Atmosphere:** A familiar impossible interior, phone glow, rain-muted silence.
- **Symbolic image:** A contract line that keeps rewriting itself as a childhood joke.
- **What changes after the scene:** Lu Mingfei understands that the final choice must transform the debt rather than dodge it.
- **Risk of mishandling:** Over-explaining Lu Mingze kills his mystery; under-explaining the price kills the payoff.

### Scene 3 — Nono refuses to become the answer

- **Scene purpose:** Clarify love without possession and protect Nono's agency.
- **Characters involved:** Nono, Lu Mingfei.
- **Emotional subtext:** She cares, but care is not ownership, rescue, or romantic compensation.
- **Atmosphere:** Moving vehicle, wet city lights, a conversation that dodges direct confession.
- **Symbolic image:** Nono driving through a red light after telling the truth indirectly.
- **What changes after the scene:** Lu Mingfei stops expecting recognition to solve loneliness.
- **Risk of mishandling:** The scene may frustrate readers if it sounds like evasive romance rather than mature boundary.

### Scene 4 — Chu writes the erased names

- **Scene purpose:** Convert memory restoration into witness action.
- **Characters involved:** Chu Zihang, Kassel archivists, Lu Mingfei or Anjou as witness.
- **Emotional subtext:** Remembering is not catharsis; it is responsibility.
- **Atmosphere:** Archive room, damp paper, cold light, distant alarms.
- **Symbolic image:** Names appearing one by one on a page that should be blank.
- **What changes after the scene:** Odin's power weakens because erasure is no longer private.
- **Risk of mishandling:** Too many names may become list-making instead of grief.

### Scene 5 — Caesar breaks the inherited table

- **Scene purpose:** Close Caesar's family/institutional arc through mature honor.
- **Characters involved:** Caesar, Secret Party figures, Gattuso representatives, Nono.
- **Emotional subtext:** He chooses to be noble by losing the privileges that taught him nobility's performance.
- **Atmosphere:** Elegant room, emergency war feed, polished surfaces under strain.
- **Symbolic image:** A cracked crest reflected in Caesar's glass.
- **What changes after the scene:** Elite factions lose their clean escape route.
- **Risk of mishandling:** Caesar could become a speech machine unless the cost lands personally.

### Scene 6 — Erii's no-return boundary

- **Scene purpose:** Establish that emotional compensation cannot reverse death.
- **Characters involved:** Lu Mingfei, Erii's memory trace, Nono or Lu Mingze as counterweight.
- **Emotional subtext:** The kindest love is refusing a false miracle.
- **Atmosphere:** Quiet sea-image, archive room, or memory-space with soft red light.
- **Symbolic image:** A preserved small object that remains only an object.
- **What changes after the scene:** Lu Mingfei rejects resurrection temptation in advance of the final choice.
- **Risk of mishandling:** Erii must not become a tool for Lu Mingfei's growth alone.

### Scene 7 — Odin's rain-night defeat

- **Scene purpose:** Pay off Chu's erased history and the memory/fate mechanism.
- **Characters involved:** Chu Zihang, Odin, Lu Mingfei, possibly Anjou.
- **Emotional subtext:** The son does not win by killing a god; he wins by making the erased world testify.
- **Atmosphere:** Highway rain, headlights, impossible horse-shadow, names cutting through static.
- **Symbolic image:** A road that no longer loops back into forgetting.
- **What changes after the scene:** Odin's inevitability breaks, enabling the final throne confrontation.
- **Risk of mishandling:** If Odin becomes too chatty, the scene loses dread.

### Scene 8 — Lu Mingfei arrives first

- **Scene purpose:** Complete Lu Mingfei's agency arc.
- **Characters involved:** Lu Mingfei, Lu Mingze, Black/White symbolic presence, distant friends.
- **Emotional subtext:** The boy who was always late chooses to stand before the bill reaches anyone else.
- **Atmosphere:** Mythic battlefield reduced to one wet threshold.
- **Symbolic image:** An empty throne reflected in puddles.
- **What changes after the scene:** The succession engine can be refused rather than inherited.
- **Risk of mishandling:** This must be choice, not martyrdom autopilot or power-up spectacle.

### Scene 9 — Lu Mingze's last laugh or last silence

- **Scene purpose:** Give Lu Mingze emotional closure without conventional survival.
- **Characters involved:** Lu Mingfei, Lu Mingze.
- **Emotional subtext:** Brotherhood may have existed inside manipulation, but neither word can fully excuse the other.
- **Atmosphere:** White quiet after impact, no crowd, no victory music.
- **Symbolic image:** A phone contact that remains but cannot be called.
- **What changes after the scene:** Lu Mingfei survives or continues without the familiar impossible rescuer.
- **Risk of mishandling:** Too sentimental makes Lu Mingze harmless; too cold wastes the bond.

### Scene 10 — The repaired classroom and empty seat

- **Scene purpose:** Deliver final aftertaste and symbolize lost youth.
- **Characters involved:** Lu Mingfei, Nono, Chu, Caesar, Kassel survivors.
- **Emotional subtext:** They saved the world but cannot re-enter the afternoon they lost.
- **Atmosphere:** Post-rain sunlight, repaired windows, school sounds returning unevenly.
- **Symbolic image:** An empty seat under a clean window.
- **What changes after the scene:** The route closes on continuation rather than restoration.
- **Risk of mishandling:** It may feel too neat unless absence remains visible.

### Scene 11 — The bounded final echo

- **Scene purpose:** Preserve open resonance without invalidating cost.
- **Characters involved:** Lu Mingfei or a survivor; possible Lu Mingze echo.
- **Emotional subtext:** Love and memory may leave traces, but traces are not rescue.
- **Atmosphere:** Quiet station, campus path, or rain-stopped street.
- **Symbolic image:** A phone rings once, then stays silent.
- **What changes after the scene:** The reader leaves with mystery that does not overturn the rules.
- **Risk of mishandling:** If the echo feels like a sequel hook or secret resurrection, it cheapens the ending.

## 8. Style strategy

### Youthful academy tone

Open the final volume with ordinary school textures: cafeteria noise, dorm corridors, bad jokes, wet uniforms, clubs, exams, student gossip, and absurd mission logistics. These details are not filler; they remind the reader what the final war is protecting and what the characters can no longer fully recover.

### Tragic emotional density

Every major answer should cost someone a future, memory, relationship, illusion, or role. The route should avoid announcing tragedy directly; it should place grief in objects, absences, changed behavior, and restrained choices.

### Epic world-ending scale

Use C's scale through faction convergence, dragon recurrence, Odin's memory field, Black/White succession, and the throne refusal. However, every spectacle beat must be paired with an individual wound: Chu's road, Lu Mingfei's bargain, Nono's freedom, Caesar's family, Erii's boundary, Anjou's old war.

### Reflective essay-like maturity

After action beats, add reflective pauses about youth, memory, and lost time. These reflections should not preach. They should feel like survivors noticing that the world continues with an indecently ordinary sound: rain gutters, classroom bells, phone static, or cafeteria trays.

### Humor before heaviness

Place fragile humor before the darkest turns: Lu Mingfei's self-mockery before the bargain reveal, Caesar's elegant confidence before family rupture, Nono's teasing before emotional boundary, student banter before mobilization. Humor should delay fear, not deny it.

### Loneliness / fate / youth / sacrifice / lost time motifs

- **Loneliness:** Lu Mingfei is seen at last but still not fully reachable.
- **Fate:** the throne is a mechanism built before him, but his refusal changes its outcome.
- **Youth:** graduation imagery marks an end, not a reset.
- **Sacrifice:** every victory specifies what cannot return.
- **Lost time:** the ending mourns ordinary days, missed calls, and people who cannot attend the repaired future.

### Action followed by emotional emptiness

After major battles, do not cut immediately to victory logistics. Show silence, broken objects, missing names, characters unable to joke, or a room that no longer fits the survivors.

### Grand conflicts expressed through individual choices

The final route should never say the world is saved only because a mythic system collapsed. It is saved because Lu Mingfei refuses the throne, Chu records the erased, Nono refuses ownership, Caesar breaks inherited immunity, Anjou's war is judged, and survivors choose accountable memory.

## 9. Consistency risks

| Risk | Prevention strategy |
| --- | --- |
| Power fantasy risk | Make Lu Mingfei's decisive act a refusal and payment, not a stronger attack. Pair every power use with a relationship, memory, or identity cost. |
| Over-explanation risk | Separate operational clarity from cosmic mystery: explain rules that determine choices, leave ancient origin and final metaphysical texture partly resonant. |
| Emotional cheapness risk | Keep no-resurrection rules firm. Compensation can be recognition, farewell, memory, and small human continuity, not restoration of lost people or effortless romance. |
| Character OOC risk | Check each major beat against voice logic: Lu Mingfei awkward/self-mocking, Lu Mingze teasing/cruel/tender, Chu restrained, Caesar grand but generous, Nono free and evasive, Anjou elegant and ruthless. |
| Worldbuilding contradiction risk | Mark succession, bargain, memory, and bloodline mechanics as route assumptions until later story consistency review. Do not introduce unexplained powers to solve the climax. |
| Pacing collapse risk | Use the 12-unit structure to alternate academy lightness, investigation, political fracture, memory confrontation, mythic reveal, final battle, aftermath, and graduation resonance. |
| Reader rejection risk | Provide enough closure on must-resolve threads while preserving one or two controlled mysteries. Make the final echo emotionally bounded so readers do not feel tricked. |
| Institutional whitewashing risk | Kassel and the Secret Party must survive only through accountability, memorial obligation, and changed rules, not nostalgia alone. |
| Tragedy overload risk | Balance loss with earned recognition, selected survivor glimpses, and small ordinary continuities so the ending hurts without feeling punitive. |
| Romance simplification risk | Keep Nono's agency central and treat Erii's memory as ethical presence, not romantic scorekeeping. |

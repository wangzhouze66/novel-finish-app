# Dragon Raja / Long Zu Style and Story Consistency Checklist v1

Use this checklist after `14_scene_level_outline_v1.md` and before any sample-scene drafting, chapter drafting, or rewrite pass. It is a review tool, not a prose-generation file. It should help reviewers decide whether an outline or sample draft still belongs to the current hybrid route and mixed target style.

This checklist assumes the active route is the hybrid route in `11_hybrid_route_v1.md`, expanded through the final-volume, chapter-level, and scene-level outlines in `12_final_volume_outline_v1.md`, `13_chapter_level_outline_v1.md`, and `14_scene_level_outline_v1.md`.

---

## 1. Review purpose

This checklist must be used **before approving any scene draft or chapter draft**.

The purpose is to prevent future writing from drifting into a generic ending, a pure battle resolution, a romance-reward structure, or a mechanical mystery explanation. Every approved scene or chapter should pass three linked tests:

1. **Style test:** Does it preserve the mixed target style: academy youth, tragic density, mythic scale, restrained emotion, humor before heaviness, and reflective aftertaste?
2. **Character test:** Do the characters still sound and choose like themselves, even under final-volume pressure?
3. **Story test:** Does the scene or chapter obey the current route logic: empty-throne refusal, witness continuity, memory justice, no cheap resurrection, no clean absolution, and aftermath with cost?

Review order:

1. Check style and emotional rhythm.
2. Check character voice and decision logic.
3. Check continuity, thread recovery, and route alignment.
4. Score the scene or chapter.
5. Mark it as **pass**, **revise**, or **reject and replan**.

Chinese planning label: `先审风格，再审人物，再审故事逻辑；未通过前不得扩写成正式章节。`

## 2. Style consistency checklist

Use this section for scenes, chapter outlines, sample drafts, and rewrite passes.

| Check item | What to confirm | Revision trigger |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Youthful academy tone when needed | School texture, awkward jokes, cafeteria/dorm/classroom details, student logistics, and ordinary warmth remain visible when the chapter calls for them. | The scene feels like only apocalypse, conspiracy, or battle, with no trace of youth or ordinary life. |
| Tragic emotional density | Loss has weight because it changes identity, memory, relationship, or future. | Tragedy appears only as shock, death count, crying, or decorative sadness. |
| Epic scale with personal anchor | Mythic conflict is tied to one promise, name, object, road, seat, phone, or decision. | The scene explains dragons, kings, factions, or fate without a human anchor. |
| Reflective aftertaste | The ending beat leaves an image, silence, regret, half-joke, changed object, or unanswered emotional question. | The scene ends only on plot information, a victory line, or cliffhanger mechanics. |
| Humor before heaviness | Humor delays fear, protects tenderness, or exposes insecurity before heavier pressure arrives. | Humor mocks grief, undercuts stakes, becomes meme dialogue, or appears after the cost should land. |
| Loneliness motif | A character can be among allies and still feel late, unseen, or separated by what they know. | Everyone feels too coordinated, emotionally satisfied, or frictionless. |
| Fate motif | Fate feels like an old mechanism, but the scene still turns on a personal choice. | Fate is either solved like a puzzle or used to remove character agency. |
| Youth motif | Youth is treated as something protected, mourned, and changed by the ending. | Youth becomes nostalgia decoration with no bearing on decisions. |
| Sacrifice motif | Sacrifice costs identity, memory, relationship, future, authority, or ordinary return. | Sacrifice works like a coupon that buys victory without aftermath. |
| Lost time motif | The scene acknowledges that some afternoons, names, people, or conversations cannot be restored. | Memory recovery behaves like a full reset or complete emotional repair. |
| Action followed by emotional emptiness | After chase, battle, or revelation, the scene shows quiet cost: empty seat, broken object, ordinary detail, silence, or unfinished call. | Action escalates into more action without a pause for consequence. |
| Restrained emotional expression | Emotion appears through gesture, silence, refusal, object focus, or sparse directness. | Characters explain their feelings in speeches, preach the theme, or confess too neatly. |
| Symbolic imagery | Rain, road, empty seat, phone, sea, classroom, city lights, or dragon outline has a clear emotional function. | Symbols appear randomly or too often, becoming decorative labels. |
| Non-mechanical mystery handling | Mysteries reveal function, cost, and emotional consequence without explaining every origin. | The scene becomes a lore lecture, technical diagram, or solved-wiki entry. |

Minimum style requirement: a scene should satisfy **at least seven** style checks, including **one rhythm check**, **one motif check**, **one restraint check**, and **one aftertaste check**.

## 3. Character voice checklist

Use this section whenever a listed character appears, is remembered, or strongly shapes the scene.

### Lu Mingfei

- **What must be preserved:** Inferiority, longing, ordinary desire, self-mockery, loyalty, hidden courage, awkward deflection, and plain sincerity under pressure. His final agency must remain human rather than imperial.
- **Common failure mode:** He becomes a clean conqueror, cool martyr, chosen emperor, or fully healed hero who no longer sounds like the boy who arrived late.
- **Revision trigger:** Any line or decision that solves the conflict by dominance alone, treats friends as rewards, or removes his self-doubt and ordinary desire.

### Lu Mingze

- **What must be preserved:** Uncanny intimacy, bargain logic, playful cruelty, brotherly ambiguity, dangerous tenderness, and the feeling that rescue and trap can be the same gesture.
- **Common failure mode:** He becomes only a villain, only a helpful brother, only a contract device, or an exposition machine explaining the bargain.
- **Revision trigger:** Any scene where his affection, manipulation, cost, and loneliness are not entangled.

### Chu Zihang

- **What must be preserved:** Discipline, guilt, duty, solitude, suppressed tenderness, practical speech, and memory as action rather than confession.
- **Common failure mode:** He becomes verbose, melodramatic, flippant, or a simple combat unit.
- **Revision trigger:** Any scene where he explains his pain at length instead of acting precisely, recording names, protecting someone, or making a controlled statement.

### Caesar

- **What must be preserved:** Pride, charisma, aristocratic loneliness, generosity, tactical intelligence, dry humor, and the ability to turn style into responsibility.
- **Common failure mode:** He becomes comic arrogance, decorative nobility, or a frictionless rich ally who solves logistics without cost.
- **Revision trigger:** Any chapter where Caesar's grandeur lacks sacrifice, accountability, or chosen loyalty.

### Nono

- **What must be preserved:** Freedom, danger, perceptiveness, teasing sharpness, emotional evasiveness, sudden clarity, mobility, and refusal to be owned.
- **Common failure mode:** She becomes Lu Mingfei's reward, emotional cure, passive rescue target, or romantic proof that he is finally chosen.
- **Revision trigger:** Any scene where her decision exists mainly to comfort Lu Mingfei rather than to express her own agency, boundary, route, or responsibility.

### Erii's emotional legacy

- **What must be preserved:** Innocence, isolation, fragile wonder, ordinary longing, dignity, and the no-return boundary. Her memory should protect tenderness without becoming resurrection bait.
- **Common failure mode:** She becomes a pain device, saintly symbol, magical solution, copy, hallucinated reward, or tool for manipulating Lu Mingfei.
- **Revision trigger:** Any scene implying that grief can be repaired by restoring, replacing, or consuming her presence.

### Principal Anjou

- **What must be preserved:** Elegance, old grief, obsession, revenge, charm, ruthlessness, strategic patience, and the burden of historical sacrifice.
- **Common failure mode:** He becomes a pure mentor, pure villain, lore lecturer, or cleanly absolved old hero.
- **Revision trigger:** Any scene where his explanation lacks blade, his methods lack cost, or his legacy escapes judgment.

### Odin

- **What must be preserved:** Fatal inevitability, erased memory, rain-road dread, mythic pursuit, and delayed revelation through consequences.
- **Common failure mode:** Odin becomes a normal boss, over-humanized antagonist, solved puzzle, or defeated monster with no memory cost.
- **Revision trigger:** Any scene explaining him more than showing his rule through absence, altered records, wrong names, isolated witnesses, or fate-loop pressure.

### Black King / White King symbolic presence

- **What must be preserved:** Origin myth, absolute power, rebellion, inheritance, inhuman scale, succession pressure, domination, purification, and the temptation of an absolute solution.
- **Common failure mode:** They become only final-boss mechanics, lore trivia, power-up sources, or combat forms.
- **Revision trigger:** Any scene where their presence does not reshape identity, fate, sacrifice, or the ethical meaning of victory.

## 4. Story continuity checklist

Use this checklist to protect the hybrid route from contradiction or accidental route drift.

| Continuity area | Required check | Revision trigger |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Timeline consistency | Events follow the final-volume phase order: return wound, gathering routes, revelation pressure, route selection, final conflict, aftermath resonance. | A scene uses information, emotional closure, or institutional outcome before the route has earned it. |
| Chapter placement | The scene's function matches its chapter and phase. | A chapter tries to do the work of a later revelation, climax, or aftermath beat too early. |
| Worldbuilding consistency | Memory erasure, witness continuity, dragon succession, faction control, and empty-throne logic follow the current route rules. | Rules shift for convenience, victory, romance, or spectacle. |
| Unresolved thread handling | Must-resolve threads receive decision, cost, or emotional closure; partial threads are marked as partial; resonant threads remain intentionally open. | A must-resolve thread is ignored, or an open thread feels forgotten rather than deliberately resonant. |
| Foreshadowing payoff | Prior images, objects, names, roads, phones, empty seats, rain, and records return with changed meaning. | A payoff appears without setup, or setup never returns in a meaningful form. |
| Relationship continuity | Lu Mingfei/Nono, Lu Mingfei/Lu Mingze, Chu/Odin, Caesar/Gattuso, Anjou/old war, and Erii's legacy retain their established boundaries. | Relationships reset, become too clean, or turn into reward/punishment mechanics. |
| Emotional arc continuity | Each character's decision grows from earlier wounds and does not skip the cost stage. | A character suddenly becomes healed, cruel, romantic, heroic, or defeated without bridge scenes. |
| Final route alignment | The scene supports empty-throne refusal, witness continuity, memory justice, constrained institutions, and ordinary life after cost. | The scene drifts into emperor succession, total restoration, simple apocalypse victory, or full romantic compensation. |
| Aftermath logic | Survival creates policy, memory, repair, silence, and changed daily life rather than instant normalcy. | The aftermath reads as reset, celebration-only ending, or sequel hook. |

Required route anchors:

- Lu Mingfei must choose agency without becoming a power-fantasy ruler.
- Lu Mingze's cost must be emotionally legible even if metaphysics remain partly mysterious.
- Odin's current threat function must close, while some origin haze may remain.
- Erii's legacy must stay protected from resurrection logic.
- Nono must retain freedom and non-possessive care.
- Chu's memory justice must restore witness continuity, not lost time.
- Caesar and Anjou must pay institutional or legacy cost.
- The Black King / White King pressure must remain mythic and ethical, not only mechanical.

## 5. Scene-level review form

Copy this form for every scene before approving it for drafting or expansion.

```markdown
## Scene review: [scene id] — [working title]

- **Scene id:**
- **Chapter id:**
- **Purpose:**
- **Emotional subtext:**
- **Character decision:**
- **Symbolic image:**
- **Style score:** [1-10]
- **Story score:** [1-10]
- **Revision needed:** [none / minor / major / reject and replan]
- **Reviewer notes:**
```

Expanded reviewer prompts:

- **Purpose:** What story job does this scene perform that no other scene performs?
- **Emotional subtext:** What is felt but not fully said?
- **Character decision:** Who chooses something irreversible, costly, or revealing?
- **Symbolic image:** What image should remain after the scene ends?
- **Style score:** Does the scene preserve rhythm, restraint, motifs, and aftertaste?
- **Story score:** Does the scene obey timeline, thread, worldbuilding, and route logic?
- **Revision needed:** Name the smallest change that would make the scene pass.
- **Reviewer notes:** Identify any red flags, missing continuity, or voice drift.

## 6. Chapter-level review form

Copy this form for every chapter outline, sample chapter, or chapter rewrite.

```markdown
## Chapter review: [chapter id] — [working title]

- **Chapter id:**
- **Chapter function:**
- **Main thread handled:**
- **Emotional beat quality:** [weak / functional / strong / excellent]
- **Character consistency:** [1-10]
- **Pacing:** [too slow / controlled / too fast / uneven]
- **Style consistency:** [1-10]
- **Story consistency:** [1-10]
- **Risk level:** [low / medium / high]
- **Required revision:**
```

Expanded reviewer prompts:

- **Chapter function:** Which final-volume phase does this chapter serve?
- **Main thread handled:** Which unresolved thread, relationship, or route anchor advances?
- **Emotional beat quality:** Does the chapter change what the reader feels, not just what the reader knows?
- **Character consistency:** Are choices and voices aligned with the character movement map?
- **Pacing:** Does the chapter alternate action, discovery, silence, and reflection with intention?
- **Style consistency:** Does the chapter keep the mixed target style rather than a single mood?
- **Story consistency:** Does the chapter protect route logic and avoid continuity shortcuts?
- **Risk level:** What is the largest danger: exposition, cheap tragedy, power fantasy, OOC voice, or rushed closure?
- **Required revision:** What must change before the chapter can proceed?

## 7. Red-flag list

If any red flag appears, pause drafting and revise the outline first.

1. **Power fantasy resolution:** A stronger power, bloodline awakening, or throne claim solves the problem without moral or emotional cost.
2. **Cheap tragedy:** Loss is used for shock, tears, or darkness without dignity, decision, or aftermath.
3. **Over-explained mystery:** Odin, Lu Mingze, dragon succession, or Black King / White King logic becomes a full mechanical lecture.
4. **Flat internet-novel dialogue:** Characters use generic taunts, meme banter, slogan-like declarations, or interchangeable voices.
5. **Emotional preaching:** The theme is explained directly instead of carried by image, choice, silence, or cost.
6. **Character OOC:** A character acts against core voice to create romance, victory, exposition, or melodrama.
7. **Worldbuilding contradiction:** Memory, witness, succession, faction, or dragon rules change only to make the scene easier.
8. **Rushed closure:** A major wound is solved in one conversation, one victory, one hug, one confession, or one explanation.
9. **No aftertaste:** The scene ends cleanly without lingering image, regret, silence, altered object, or emotional question.
10. **Action without emotional cost:** Combat, chase, or spectacle leaves no absence, fatigue, changed relationship, or ordinary detail made strange.

Red-flag Chinese shorthand: `爽文胜利、廉价悲剧、谜题讲完、人物走样、没有余味。`

## 8. Scoring system

Score each category from **1 to 10**. Use half-points only if the reviewer needs finer distinction.

| Score range | Meaning |
| --- | --- |
| 1-2 | Fails the requirement; likely breaks style, character, or route logic. |
| 3-4 | Has the intended idea but creates serious drift or contradiction. |
| 5-6 | Functional but unstable; usable only after targeted revision. |
| 7-8 | Solid; consistent enough for the next workflow stage with minor edits. |
| 9-10 | Strong; preserves style, voice, route logic, and aftertaste under pressure. |

Required scoring categories:

| Category | What a high score means |
| --- | --- |
| Style consistency | Mixed target style is clear: academy warmth, tragedy, epic scale, restraint, humor placement, and reflective aftertaste. |
| Character consistency | Voices, choices, boundaries, and emotional evasions match the character checklist. |
| Story logic | Timeline, worldbuilding, thread recovery, and final-route alignment are coherent. |
| Emotional power | The scene or chapter hurts, warms, or unsettles through earned cost rather than direct preaching. |
| Symbolic imagery | Images such as rain, road, phone, empty seat, classroom, sea, or dragon outline carry plot and emotion. |
| Pacing | Action, discovery, dialogue, silence, and reflection appear in the right order and proportion. |
| Aftertaste | The ending beat lingers through image, absence, joke, silence, or changed ordinary object. |
| Expansion readiness | The outline is specific enough to draft without inventing new core logic. |

## 9. Pass / revise / reject rules

### Pass: can move to drafting or next outline stage

A scene or chapter can pass when all conditions are true:

- Average score is **7.5 or higher**.
- No category is below **7**.
- No red flag is present.
- The scene has a clear purpose, decision, emotional subtext, and symbolic image.
- The chapter or scene supports the hybrid route rather than a different ending type.
- Character voice is stable for every major character present.

### Revise: needs another outline or rewrite pass

A scene or chapter needs revision when any condition is true:

- Average score is **5.5 to 7.4**.
- One or two categories are below **7**, but the core idea still fits the route.
- A red flag appears in a fixable form.
- The scene has emotional intent but lacks story function, or has story function but lacks aftertaste.
- Dialogue, exposition, or pacing can be repaired without changing the scene's role.

Revision should target the weakest category first. Do not expand the prose until the reviewer can name the scene's decision, cost, and final image.

### Reject and replan: do not draft yet

A scene or chapter should be rejected and replanned when any condition is true:

- Average score is **below 5.5**.
- Any category is **3 or below**.
- It breaks final-route logic, worldbuilding rules, or a must-resolve thread.
- It turns Lu Mingfei into a power-fantasy ruler or passive payment.
- It turns Nono into a reward, Erii into resurrection bait, Lu Mingze into a simple device, or Odin into a solved boss.
- It creates cheap closure that contradicts the final-volume emotional curve.
- The reviewer cannot identify a necessary reason for the scene to exist.

Rejecting a scene is not failure; it protects the outline-first workflow from forcing a bad draft.

## 10. Next-step recommendation

Recommended next task: **create a revision loop prompt**.

Reason: the scene-level outline already identifies fragile scenes and the current milestone now has a review checklist. A reusable revision loop prompt should apply this checklist to one selected scene or chapter, produce targeted fixes, and keep analysis, revision, and drafting separate.

Suggested next-step order:

1. **Create revision loop prompt** for reviewing a scene or chapter against this checklist.
2. **Revise scene-level outline** for any scene that scores below pass level.
3. **Fill character cards** only where the checklist exposes missing voice or relationship constraints.
4. **Draft one sample scene** only after the selected scene passes review.
5. **Create sample chapter draft** only after multiple scenes in that chapter pass scene-level review.

Do **not** create full chapters or final prose until at least one sample scene has passed style, character, and story review.

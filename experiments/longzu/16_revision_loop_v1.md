# Dragon Raja / Long Zu Revision Loop v1

This document defines the first reusable revision loop for future sample scenes and chapter drafts in the _Dragon Raja / Long Zu_ ending experiment. It is a **process document**, not a scene draft, not final prose, and not a rewrite of any existing outline.

Active route context: `Hybrid Route v1 — 雨后王座无人 / The Empty Throne After Rain`.

Core rule: `任何场景都不能一稿通过。先诊断，再改写；先守住人物和故事逻辑，再追求句子漂亮。`

---

## 1. Revision loop purpose

No scene or chapter should be accepted after one draft. A raw draft is only a test object: it reveals whether the selected outline, character movement, emotional beat, and symbolic image can survive prose pressure.

Every draft must pass through four review layers before approval:

1. **Style review:** checks whether the draft preserves the mixed target style: youthful academy tone, tragic emotional density, epic scale, reflective maturity, emotional restraint, humor before heaviness, loneliness, fate, youth, sacrifice, and lost time motifs.
2. **Story review:** checks timeline, worldbuilding, unresolved thread handling, foreshadowing payoff, final-route alignment, and whether closure is earned rather than mechanical.
3. **Character review:** checks whether each character sounds emotionally right, acts from established wounds and desires, avoids OOC drift, and remains more than a plot tool.
4. **Emotional-aftertaste review:** checks whether the draft leaves a lingering image, silence, changed relationship, ordinary detail, or ache rather than clean explanation, easy victory, or cheap tragedy.

The loop exists to prevent the most likely failure modes in later drafting:

- character OOC
- flat dialogue
- dry plot summary
- emotional overstatement
- mechanical mystery explanation
- cheap tragedy
- power fantasy drift
- weak aftertaste
- worldbuilding contradictions
- unresolved threads being closed too mechanically

## 2. Revision stages

### Stage 0. Pre-draft alignment

Before writing or generating any draft, align the scene with the existing planning files.

Check:

- The selected chapter outline in `13_chapter_level_outline_v1.md`.
- The selected scene outline in `14_scene_level_outline_v1.md`.
- The related character cards in `02_character_cards.md`.
- The related unresolved threads in `05_unresolved_threads.md`.
- The target style notes in `07_author_style_bible.md`.
- The final route logic in `11_hybrid_route_v1.md`.
- The final-volume position in `12_final_volume_outline_v1.md`.
- The review constraints in `15_style_story_consistency_checklist_v1.md`.

Why this exists: a scene should not invent its own route logic. It should test and deepen the already-selected route.

### Stage 1. Draft v0

Generate or write a rough scene/chapter draft.

Focus on:

- structure
- scene purpose
- emotional direction
- character movement
- the central choice or pressure point
- the image that should remain after the scene ends

Do not expect final polish. Draft v0 is allowed to be uneven, but it must not ignore the outline, change the route premise, create final prose, or skip emotional cost.

### Stage 2. Style review

Check whether the draft preserves the target style instead of sliding into generic genre prose.

Review for:

- mixed target style: school-life lightness, tragic density, epic scale, and reflective maturity
- emotional restraint instead of direct preaching
- dialogue rhythm and character-specific speech texture
- symbolic imagery that carries theme without lecturing
- humor before heaviness where contrast is appropriate
- action followed by emotional emptiness, fatigue, or silence
- reflective aftertaste rather than clean triumph
- avoidance of flat internet-novel tone, slogan lines, meme banter, and interchangeable voices

Why this exists: the ending should feel like a rain-washed graduation after catastrophe, not a mechanics summary or power escalation report.

### Stage 3. Story consistency review

Check whether the draft obeys the route, timeline, and continuity constraints.

Review for:

- timeline consistency
- worldbuilding rules around bloodline, succession, memory, witness networks, institutional power, and secrecy
- unresolved thread handling without rushed closure
- foreshadowing payoff through image, choice, consequence, or remembered detail
- relationship continuity
- final route alignment with empty-throne refusal, witness continuity, memory justice, constrained institutions, and ordinary life after irreversible cost

Why this exists: mystery can remain resonant, but story logic must be fair.

### Stage 4. Character voice review

Check each relevant character through the character-card lens:

- Lu Mingfei
- Lu Mingze
- Chu Zihang
- Caesar
- Nono
- Erii's emotional legacy
- Principal Anjou
- Odin
- Black King / White King symbolic presence

For each relevant character or presence, check:

- whether the character sounds emotionally right
- whether they are avoiding or directly saying the right things for their personality
- whether their choices fit Hybrid Route v1
- whether they have become a plot tool, exposition device, romance reward, grief button, or final-boss mechanic

Why this exists: an ending route can change outcomes, but it should not break core voice identity.

### Stage 5. Problem diagnosis

Classify every major problem before rewriting. Do not jump straight to sentence polishing.

Problem types:

- **Style problem:** tone, rhythm, imagery, restraint, humor placement, or prose texture does not match the target.
- **Character problem:** a character speaks, chooses, reacts, or avoids emotion in a way that breaks the card.
- **Plot logic problem:** cause/effect, timeline, stakes, or scene premise does not hold.
- **Pacing problem:** the draft rushes, stalls, over-explains, skips aftermath, or spends too long on low-value action.
- **Worldbuilding problem:** dragon, memory, witness, bloodline, institutional, or succession rules contradict the route.
- **Emotional texture problem:** feelings are too flat, too shouted, too sentimental, or not connected to prior desire and cost.
- **Scene-function problem:** the scene does not perform a unique story job.
- **Symbolic-image problem:** images are absent, decorative, too obvious, or disconnected from emotional movement.
- **Aftertaste problem:** the ending lands too cleanly, triumphantly, mechanically, or without lingering resonance.

Why this exists: accurate diagnosis prevents superficial revision.

### Stage 6. Rewrite brief

Before rewriting, produce a precise rewrite brief.

The brief must state:

- what to keep
- what to cut
- what to deepen
- what to make more restrained
- what to make more concrete
- what emotional subtext to add
- what dialogue to reduce
- what symbolic image to strengthen
- what character logic to repair

Why this exists: the rewrite should have a target, not just a vague instruction to “make it better.”

### Stage 7. Draft v1

Rewrite using the brief.

Do not merely polish sentences. Repair:

- structure
- subtext
- character voice
- emotional movement
- scene purpose
- image pattern
- story logic
- aftertaste

Why this exists: v1 should be a structural and emotional improvement, not only a smoother version of the same failed scene.

### Stage 8. Delta review

Compare v0 and v1 directly.

Review:

- what improved
- what became worse
- what still fails
- whether the scene is closer to the target style
- whether the story logic is stronger
- whether the emotional aftertaste improved

Why this exists: revision can introduce new problems. The delta review protects against false improvement.

### Stage 9. Pass / revise / reject decision

Make one of three decisions:

- **Pass:** The scene can move to the next scene or next workflow stage. Minor polish is allowed later, but style, story, character, and aftertaste are stable.
- **Revise:** The scene premise is usable, but another rewrite brief is required. Do not proceed until the highest-risk problems are repaired.
- **Reject:** The scene premise is wrong and must be replanned from the outline level. Do not keep polishing a premise that breaks character, story logic, route alignment, or emotional truth.

Why this exists: some drafts need revision; some need replanning. The loop must be honest about the difference.

## 6. Style-specific revision rules

Use these rules whenever a draft feels functional but tonally wrong.

1. **If the scene feels too explanatory,** convert explanation into image, memory, silence, or conflict.
2. **If the dialogue feels too direct,** add avoidance, humor, interruption, misdirection, unfinished sentences, or subtext.
3. **If the battle feels empty,** attach it to a character choice, visible cost, relationship change, or emotional consequence.
4. **If the tragedy feels cheap,** connect it to prior desire, memory, sacrifice, dignity, and aftermath.
5. **If mystery becomes mechanical,** leave partial shadow and emphasize emotional consequence.
6. **If closure feels too easy,** add cost, loss, distance, public accountability, or irreversible change.
7. **If a scene lacks aftertaste,** end with ordinary detail, silence, weather, memory, damaged school-life texture, or a changed relationship.
8. **If the prose becomes one-tone solemn,** restore contrast through awkward academy detail, dry humor, logistical inconvenience, or a small human reaction before the weight returns.
9. **If symbolism becomes decorative,** tie the image to a choice, absence, debt, wound, or changed future.
10. **If emotion is overstated,** remove explanatory feeling words and let gesture, object, weather, or unfinished dialogue carry the ache.

Chinese shorthand: `少解释，多留白；少喊痛，多让物件和沉默作证。`

## 7. Character-specific revision triggers

Use these triggers to decide when a draft must be revised for voice, agency, or emotional truth.

### Lu Mingfei

Revise if:

- Lu Mingfei becomes a pure heroic power fantasy figure.
- His final strength is framed as domination, throne ownership, or effortless bloodline superiority.
- He states his feelings too cleanly without awkwardness, self-mockery, avoidance, or delayed recognition.
- His loneliness is solved by romance, victory, resurrection, or public praise.
- He becomes passive while other characters solve his route choice for him.

Revision direction: return him to reluctant agency, ordinary-boy residue, self-deprecating humor, painful recognition, and the refusal to let lonely children become payment.

### Lu Mingze

Revise if:

- Lu Mingze explains himself too plainly.
- He becomes only a contract mechanic, cheat code, villain, or sentimental younger brother.
- The bargain becomes a clean transaction without love, coercion, cost, and ambiguity.
- His dialogue loses teasing sharpness, theatrical control, or hidden fear.
- His final cost is treated as a puzzle answer rather than an emotional wound.

Revision direction: keep partial mystery, make motive emotionally legible through behavior, and let jokes turn into wounds rather than lectures.

### Chu Zihang

Revise if:

- Chu Zihang becomes only a plot device for Odin.
- His memory wound is solved by information alone.
- He speaks with excessive emotional explanation instead of controlled action, restraint, and burdened precision.
- Revenge replaces witness duty.
- His recovered truth gives him easy peace.

Revision direction: make him a carrier of memory justice, not a solved victim. His silence should have pressure, and his choices should show what knowledge costs.

### Caesar

Revise if:

- Caesar loses pride, elegance, and burden.
- He becomes comic arrogance without generosity, tactical intelligence, or noble loneliness.
- His institutional sacrifice costs him nothing concrete.
- He solves elite corruption through one stylish gesture with no aftermath.
- His dialogue becomes generic bravado.

Revision direction: preserve grandeur under accountability. Let wit precede consequence, and make the price of breaking inherited power visible.

### Nono

Revise if:

- Nono becomes only romantic closure.
- She acts as emotional medicine, reward, or explanation tool for Lu Mingfei.
- She loses freedom, danger, perceptiveness, or evasive warmth.
- Her care becomes possession, confession, or simple rescue.
- She stops making consequential choices of her own.

Revision direction: protect non-possessive care, mobility, teasing sharpness, and agency. Her warmth should remain difficult to capture.

### Erii's emotional legacy

Revise if:

- Erii's legacy becomes cheap emotional manipulation.
- Her memory is used only to make Lu Mingfei suffer.
- The draft implies easy resurrection, replacement, or counterfeit compensation.
- Her ordinary desires are forgotten in favor of tragedy spectacle.
- Her absence stops functioning as an ethical boundary.

Revision direction: honor tenderness, small ordinary dreams, and no-resurrection ethics. Her legacy should protect the value of the ordinary world, not become a grief lever.

### Principal Anjou

Revise if:

- Principal Anjou becomes a simple mentor, benevolent administrator, or exposition machine.
- His elegance, irony, ruthlessness, revenge, and historical burden flatten into plot delivery.
- He explains worldbuilding without emotional or moral cost.
- His institutional choices carry no accountability.
- His old grief is ignored or sentimentalized.

Revision direction: keep charm and danger together. Explanation should feel like a blade, a debt, or a confession made under pressure.

### Odin

Revise if:

- Odin is over-humanized too early.
- Odin explains his own rules in a lecture.
- His threat becomes only a monster fight.
- Memory erasure loses emotional consequence.
- The draft resolves him without witness cost or Chu's burden.

Revision direction: keep Odin as omen, fate pressure, erased memory, and pursuit. Reveal function through effects, repeated roads, missing records, witness names, and the fear of being forgotten.

### Black King / White King symbolic presence

Revise if:

- The Black King or White King presence becomes final-boss mechanics only.
- Their symbolism is reduced to power level, lineage trivia, or lore dump.
- The dragon throne feels like a prize rather than a domination mechanism.
- The route drifts toward coronation, purification, or bloodline destiny as salvation.
- Human cost disappears beneath mythology.

Revision direction: treat them as mythic pressure on fate, identity, inheritance, domination, rebellion, and sacrifice. Their presence should make Lu Mingfei's refusal more meaningful, not more powerful.

## 8. Revision scoring system

Score each category from **1 to 10**.

| Category                     | Question                                                                                                          |
| ---------------------------- | ----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Style consistency**        | Does the draft preserve mixed target style, restraint, rhythm, humor placement, imagery, and reflective maturity? |
| **Character voice**          | Do the relevant characters sound and choose according to their cards?                                             |
| **Story continuity**         | Does the draft obey timeline, worldbuilding rules, unresolved threads, and route alignment?                       |
| **Emotional restraint**      | Does the draft avoid preaching, melodrama, and overstated feeling?                                                |
| **Emotional power**          | Does the scene actually hurt, move, unsettle, or reveal something?                                                |
| **Symbolic imagery**         | Does the scene use image as emotional structure rather than decoration?                                           |
| **Pacing**                   | Does the scene balance setup, action, silence, discovery, and aftermath?                                          |
| **Scene function**           | Does the scene perform a unique necessary job?                                                                    |
| **Aftertaste**               | Does the ending leave lingering resonance, absence, ordinary detail, or changed relationship?                     |
| **Readiness for next stage** | Can the draft move forward without breaking the route?                                                            |

Decision bands:

| Score range | Decision meaning                                                                                      |
| ----------- | ----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **8–10**    | Pass or minor polish. The draft is stable enough for the next workflow stage.                         |
| **6–7**     | Revise. The premise may work, but targeted repair is required.                                        |
| **4–5**     | Major rewrite. The draft has serious drift, contradiction, weak voice, or poor aftertaste.            |
| **1–3**     | Reject and replan. The scene premise likely breaks style, character, story logic, or route alignment. |

Minimum approval rule: a scene should not pass if any of **character voice**, **story continuity**, **emotional restraint**, or **aftertaste** scores below 7, even if other categories are strong.

Required workflow:

Usage warning: do not use this document to skip outlining. It is a revision control layer for drafts that already have chapter and scene-level planning support.

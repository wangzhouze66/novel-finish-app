# Long Zu Corpus Ingestion Guide

## Purpose

This folder is the evidence layer for the *Dragon Raja / Long Zu* ending system. Its job is to prevent route design from relying on memory, character-name familiarity, or broad impressions. Before sample scenes, final prose, or route rewrites, the system should collect structured source evidence and convert it into story, style, foreshadowing, and interpretation artifacts.

## Ingestion Principles

1. **Summarize before interpreting.** Record what the material says before deciding what it means for an ending.
2. **Separate evidence types.** Keep plot, character, worldbuilding, foreshadowing, style, author commentary, reader debate, and user interpretation in separate notes.
3. **Prefer short, source-grounded entries.** Each note should identify where the evidence came from and why it matters.
4. **Track uncertainty.** Mark contradictions, missing context, fan debate, translation uncertainty, and user-preference assumptions.
5. **Do not draft continuation prose during ingestion.** The output of this layer is structured evidence, not scenes or final chapters.

## Step-by-Step Workflow

### Step 1: Register Sources

Add every material item to `01_source_inventory.md` before summarizing it. Include novels, volume summaries, chapter summaries, official extras, author essays, interviews, adaptations, reader debates, and user interpretation notes.

Why this step exists: source tracking makes later claims auditable and keeps high-relevance materials from being mixed with lower-confidence impressions.

### Step 2: Add Novel Volume Summaries

Use `02_volume_summaries.md` to summarize each volume at the structural level. Focus on central conflict, major events, character changes, worldbuilding additions, foreshadowing, unresolved questions, emotional tone, style features, and ending relevance.

Why this step exists: volume summaries create the long-form arc map needed before choosing ending routes.

### Step 3: Add Chapter Summaries

Use `03_chapter_summaries.md` for chapter-level entries when detailed continuity is needed. Record plot function, characters present, emotional beat, worldbuilding information, foreshadowing, unresolved questions, style observations, and ending relevance.

Why this step exists: chapter summaries preserve fine-grained causality, setup/payoff timing, and emotional rhythm.

### Step 4: Collect Character Evidence

Use `04_character_evidence.md` for character-specific evidence. Record actions, dialogue tendencies, emotional patterns, hidden desires or fears, relationship signals, contradictions, and ending implications.

Why this step exists: character endings should grow from repeated behavioral evidence, not from isolated impressions.

### Step 5: Collect Worldbuilding Evidence

Use `05_worldbuilding_evidence.md` for rules, concepts, factions, powers, mythology, bloodline constraints, historical claims, contradictions, and gaps.

Why this step exists: epic-scale endings must obey the story world's logic or clearly explain any revision.

### Step 6: Collect Foreshadowing Evidence

Use `06_foreshadowing_evidence.md` for setups, motifs, symbolic details, prophecies, mysteries, repeated images, and unresolved promises.

Why this step exists: foreshadowing recovery is one of the main tests of ending quality.

### Step 7: Add Author Essay Notes

Use `07_author_essay_notes.md` for Jiangnan essays and related writing. Track core ideas, emotional attitude, views of youth, loneliness, fate, memory, loss, narrative habits, and possible rules for a *Dragon Raja / Long Zu* ending.

Why this step exists: author-style consistency requires more than surface imitation; it needs worldview, emotional restraint, and recurring narrative instincts.

### Step 8: Add Interview Notes

Use `08_interview_notes.md` for interviews and public statements. Separate summary from interpretation, and mark whether each statement is about creative intention, characters, worldbuilding, ending relevance, or style relevance.

Why this step exists: interviews can clarify intent, but they should not override textual evidence without review.

### Step 9: Add Style Evidence

Use `09_style_evidence.md` for observed style features. Record sentence rhythm, dialogue rhythm, humor pattern, tragedy pattern, imagery, emotional restraint, scene construction, and rewrite rules.

Why this step exists: later Chinese-language prompt outputs need concrete style constraints rather than vague labels.

### Step 10: Add Cultural Core Notes

Use `10_cultural_core_notes.md` to extract the emotional and cultural center of the work. Track themes such as youth, fate, sacrifice, memory, loneliness, modern fantasy, and mythology.

Why this step exists: endings should satisfy the emotional contract of the work, not only close plot mechanics.

### Step 11: Add Reader Debate Notes

Use `11_reader_debate_notes.md` for recurring reader arguments, dissatisfaction points, desired closure, risks if ignored, and possible route solutions.

Why this step exists: reader debate can reveal which unresolved promises carry the highest emotional pressure.

### Step 12: Add User Interpretation Notes

User interpretation notes may be recorded in the relevant evidence files or in a future dedicated file if the interpretation layer grows. Mark them clearly as user preference or user reading rather than source canon.

Why this step exists: the final route should respect the user's desired balance of closure, tragedy, epic scale, open resonance, and emotional compensation without confusing preference with source evidence.

### Step 13: Convert Corpus into Bibles and Routes

Use `12_corpus_to_bible_pipeline.md` after enough evidence has been collected. Update the story bible, author style bible, unresolved thread list, route candidates, hybrid route, final volume outline, chapter outline, and scene outline in that order.

Why this step exists: corpus ingestion becomes useful only when converted into planning constraints and route decisions.

## Readiness Checklist Before Sample Scenes

Before drafting any sample scene, confirm:

- [ ] Source inventory contains the major source categories.
- [ ] Volume summaries cover the relevant story span.
- [ ] Key chapters for the planned scene have been summarized.
- [ ] Major characters in the planned scene have evidence entries.
- [ ] Relevant worldbuilding rules and contradictions are documented.
- [ ] Relevant foreshadowing items have payoff possibilities.
- [ ] Style evidence includes rhythm, humor, tragedy, imagery, and restraint notes.
- [ ] Author essays or interviews have been checked when available.
- [ ] User interpretation and ending preference notes are separated from canon evidence.
- [ ] Existing routes and outlines have been updated or explicitly marked as provisional v0 scaffolding.

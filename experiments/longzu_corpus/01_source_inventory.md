# Source Inventory

## Purpose

Track every source before using it as evidence for style, plot, character, worldbuilding, foreshadowing, or ending design. Add one row per source item or source segment.

## Source Tracking Table

| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| SRC-0001 | Novel volume / chapter / essay / interview / reader debate / user interpretation | Title or description | Volume, chapter range, publication period, or discussion date | Not collected / collected / partial / unavailable | Not summarized / in progress / summarized / needs review | High / medium / low / unknown | High / medium / low / unknown | High / medium / low / unknown | Add provenance, uncertainty, translation notes, and follow-up tasks. |

## Status Definitions

- **material_status**: whether the source material has been obtained or excerpted enough for legal, private, summary-level analysis.
- **summary_status**: whether the source has been converted into structured notes.
- **style_relevance**: how useful the source is for voice, rhythm, imagery, humor, tragedy, and emotional restraint.
- **plot_relevance**: how useful the source is for causality, conflict, continuity, and worldbuilding.
- **ending_relevance**: how useful the source is for unresolved promises, route design, payoff, closure, or open resonance.

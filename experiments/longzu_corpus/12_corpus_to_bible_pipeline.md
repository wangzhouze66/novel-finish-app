# Corpus-to-Bible Pipeline

## Purpose

This pipeline explains how to convert the evidence files in `experiments/longzu_corpus/` into updated planning artifacts. Do not skip directly from corpus notes to sample scenes.

## Pipeline Overview

```text
source inventory
  ↓
volume summaries + chapter summaries
  ↓
character evidence + worldbuilding evidence + foreshadowing evidence
  ↓
author essay notes + interview notes + style evidence
  ↓
cultural core notes + reader debate notes + user interpretation notes
  ↓
updated story bible
  ↓
updated author style bible
  ↓
updated unresolved thread list
  ↓
updated ending route candidates
  ↓
updated hybrid route
  ↓
updated final volume outline
  ↓
updated chapter outline
  ↓
updated scene outline
```

## 1. Convert Corpus into Updated Story Bible

Use volume summaries, chapter summaries, character evidence, relationship signals, worldbuilding evidence, and contradictions to update the story bible.

Include:

- Current story state
- Major arcs and turning points
- Character positions and unresolved movements
- Faction and worldbuilding constraints
- Timeline and causality notes
- Continuity contradictions and uncertainty markers

Why this step exists: the story bible becomes the continuity authority for route generation.

## 2. Convert Corpus into Updated Author Style Bible

Use author essay notes, interview notes, style evidence, cultural core notes, and emotionally important chapter observations to update the author style bible.

Include:

- Voice and tonal range
- Sentence rhythm and scene rhythm
- Humor placement before heaviness
- Tragedy pattern and emotional restraint
- Recurring imagery and motifs
- Dialogue tendencies
- Rules for Chinese-language prompt outputs

Why this step exists: the style bible turns scattered observations into repeatable drafting constraints.

## 3. Convert Corpus into Updated Unresolved Thread List

Use foreshadowing evidence, unresolved questions in volume and chapter summaries, reader debate notes, and user interpretation notes to update the unresolved thread list.

Classify each thread as:

- Must resolve
- Should resolve
- Optional open resonance
- Character-emotional thread
- Worldbuilding logic thread
- Symbolic or motif thread
- Reader-pressure thread

Why this step exists: ending routes need a shared checklist of promises before they diverge.

## 4. Convert Corpus into Updated Ending Route Candidates

Use the updated story bible, style bible, unresolved thread list, cultural core notes, and ending preferences to revise route candidates.

For each candidate, update:

- Core theme
- Emotional tone
- Main conflict resolution
- Character endings
- Worldbuilding resolution
- Foreshadowing recovery
- Reader satisfaction risks
- Style risks
- Continuity risks

Why this step exists: route candidates should be evidence-driven, not only concept-driven.

## 5. Convert Route Candidates into Updated Hybrid Route

Compare candidate strengths and weaknesses. Combine the strongest elements without creating tonal contradiction or continuity conflict.

Check:

- Closure versus open resonance
- Tragic cost versus emotional compensation
- Epic scale versus intimate character payoff
- Foreshadowing recovery versus mystery preservation
- User preference versus source evidence

Why this step exists: the hybrid route should emerge from tradeoff analysis rather than default compromise.

## 6. Convert Hybrid Route into Updated Final Volume Outline

Expand the chosen or provisional hybrid into a final volume outline only after story, style, and unresolved-thread updates are complete.

Include:

- Volume-level act structure
- Major conflict escalation
- Character arc endpoints
- Worldbuilding reveals and costs
- Foreshadowing payoffs
- Emotional rhythm across the volume

Why this step exists: a final volume outline controls pacing before chapter planning.

## 7. Convert Final Volume Outline into Updated Chapter Outline

Break the final volume outline into chapter-level structure.

For each chapter, define:

- Plot function
- Character movement
- Emotional beat
- Worldbuilding information
- Foreshadowing payoff or setup
- Style target
- Continuity checks

Why this step exists: chapter outlines bridge route architecture and scene construction.

## 8. Convert Chapter Outline into Updated Scene Outline

Create scene outlines only after the chapter outline has passed consistency review.

For each scene, define:

- Scene purpose
- Characters present
- Point of view or focal center
- Conflict and turn
- Emotional restraint rule
- Image or motif use
- Dialogue function
- Continuity risk

Why this step exists: scene outlines prepare controlled drafting without jumping to final prose.

## Corpus Readiness Gate

If the corpus files are still mostly empty, treat all existing Long Zu routes and outlines as provisional v0 scaffolding. Do not treat them as final creative truth, and do not draft sample scenes from them without explicit user approval.

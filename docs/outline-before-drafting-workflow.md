# Outline-Before-Drafting Workflow

## Purpose

The system should not jump from story notes directly into full final chapters. The current priority is multi-ending architecture, outline generation, style consistency, and evaluation.

Full prose drafting should happen only after a route has passed route comparison, consistency review, and chapter-level planning.

## Workflow overview

```text
1. Story bible and unresolved thread list
        ↓
2. Multiple ending routes
        ↓
3. Route comparison matrix
        ↓
4. Hybrid route generation or route revision
        ↓
5. Final volume outline
        ↓
6. Chapter-by-chapter outline
        ↓
7. Scene-level outline
        ↓
8. Style-sensitive outline expansion
        ↓
9. Full prose drafting only after approval
```

## Step 1: Story bible and unresolved thread list

Collect and organize the core creative research artifacts: corpus map, author style bible, story bible, unresolved thread list, foreshadowing list, motifs, relationship map, timeline, and world rules.

Why this exists: ending routes need stable source constraints.

## Step 2: Generate multiple ending routes

Generate at least five routes:

- Complete closure ending
- Tragic fate ending
- Epic battle ending
- Open resonance ending
- Emotional compensation ending

Why this exists: multiple routes reveal creative range and prevent premature commitment.

## Step 3: Compare routes

Use the ending comparison matrix to score completeness, emotional power, tragedy level, reader satisfaction, story logic, character consistency, worldbuilding consistency, style consistency, originality, and expansion potential.

Why this exists: the best ending is not always the most dramatic ending.

## Step 4: Hybridize or revise

If no single route is strong enough, merge two or more routes, such as A + B, B + D, A + C, or D + E.

Why this exists: long-form endings often need both closure and emotional ambiguity.

## Step 5: Create final volume outline

Turn the selected route into a final volume plan with major movements, escalation, revelations, reversals, climax, aftermath, and thematic final image.

Why this exists: a route is a concept; a volume outline is a publishable structure.

## Step 6: Create chapter-by-chapter outline

Break the final volume into chapters. Each chapter should include plot function, point-of-view focus, emotional beat, conflict, character movement, foreshadowing payoff, and ending hook.

Why this exists: chapter planning catches pacing and continuity issues before prose exists.

## Step 7: Create scene-level outlines

Break each chapter into scenes with goals, conflict, sensory anchors, emotional turns, and transitions.

Why this exists: scene outlines make prose drafting controlled and consistent.

## Step 8: Expand outlines with style sensitivity

Add delicate emotional description, symbolic motifs, dialogue intentions, action aftermath, and reflective notes without writing full final prose.

Why this exists: style can be tested before the system commits to chapter text.

## Step 9: Draft full prose only after approval

Only draft full chapters after the user approves the route, volume outline, chapter outline, scene outline, and style direction.

Why this exists: full prose is expensive to revise and can accidentally hide structural weakness behind polished language.
